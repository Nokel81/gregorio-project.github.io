History of the Gregorio project

The Gregorio project was born in 2006 at TELECOM Bretagne, a graduate engineering school in France. It was at first a student project of six months duration, supervised by Mr Yannis Haralambous, developer of Omega. When the project was done, Élie Roux decided to continue the project on his own and to develop it under GPL.

From the outset the goal of the project has been to create a graphical interface for the monks of the Abbey of Sainte Madeleine du Barroux so that they could use a gregorian font. This font, Gregoria, is a professional font designed by Elena Albertoni, a typographer and graphic designer. Finally, due to licence issues, it was decided that the project would have its own font called gregorio.

At the end of the year 2006, a new developer, Olivier Berten, joined the project and created its OpusTeX component (deprecated since). By April 2007, Gregorio had reached a certain maturity and could start to be used, at least through its command line interface, as a preprocessor for OpusTeX. A project page was created at gna.org (now superceded by this site).

The development of the graphical interface and the modification of the code structure to use autotools and dynamic libraries was the subject of Élie's third-year project at TELECOM Bretagne. The graphical interface proved to be too complex and was never finished.

During a three-month internship starting in April 2008 at the Monastero di San Benedetto, in Norcia (Italy), Gregorio made considerable progress. The GregorioTeX package was almost finished, and as a consequence, we made progress in code stabilization.

As of 2015, three major versions of the Gregorio software have been released.

The logo

The icon is part of a picture from a Gradual of the Premonstratensian order dated 1910. It represents King David. It is available in PNG (logo.png) or SVG (logo.svg) image format.
